# Executive Summary

The United States recycles just 21% of its residential recyclable material. Of the 79% that is lost, 76% never leaves the household. The problem is not a broken sorting facility or contaminated loads. It is inertia at the source.

Scrappr is a two-sided gig economy marketplace that removes that inertia for one of the most recoverable and highest-value categories in the waste stream: scrap metal, including aluminum cans, copper, brass, steel, and large appliances.

Residents (Scrappees) photograph and list unwanted metal items. Independent haulers (Scrapprs) browse nearby listings, claim pickups, and deliver metal to local scrap yards where they are paid based on weight and material grade. Scrappees pay nothing. Scrapprs earn real income. Scrappr monetizes through hauler subscriptions and yard partnership revenue sharing.

The business model requires no municipal contracts, no landfill tipping fees, and no subsidy. The marketplace is self-funding because scrap metal has genuine commodity value. A Scrappr hauler driving a route of copper wire, aluminum cans, and a water heater can generate meaningful income in a half-day run. That economic reality is what makes Scrappr structurally different from every recycling program that has come before it.

## The Problem

### Recycling Fails at the Household Level

According to a 2024 report by The Recycling Partnership, only 21% of U.S. residential recyclable material is captured. Of the 79% lost, 76 percentage points are lost before the material ever reaches a collection bin. Contamination at sorting facilities, the problem most often cited in public discourse, accounts for just 3% of total loss.

The implication is clear: fixing sorting facilities does not fix recycling. Fixing household access and incentive does.

| Where Recyclables Are Lost                              | Share of Total Loss |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Lost at the household (never placed in a bin)           | 76%                 |
| Lost at the sorting facility (contamination, rejection) | 3%                  |
| Actually recycled                                       | 21%                 |

### The Multifamily Gap

The access problem is disproportionately concentrated in multifamily housing. Only 37% of multifamily homes have access to recycling service, compared to 85% of single-family homes. This means approximately 20 million apartment households are effectively excluded from the recycling system entirely.

Apartment residents generate scrap metal at the same rate as single-family homeowners. Old appliances, copper pipe from unit renovations, aluminum window frames, and accumulated cans represent significant recoverable value sitting in hallways, storage rooms, and dumpster areas because no convenient removal path exists.

### The Specific Problem with Scrap Metal

Scrap metal presents a unique disposal challenge that existing systems handle poorly:

- Bulky items like appliances do not fit in recycling bins
- Municipal bulk pickup is infrequent, not on-demand, and pays the resident nothing
- Drop-off recycling centers require vehicle access and effort many residents do not have
- Illegal dumping is common precisely because legitimate disposal is inconvenient

The result is that material with genuine commodity value, copper at $4.80 per pound, aluminum cans at $0.65 per pound, appliances paying $5 to $18 flat rate at local yards, ends up in landfills where it generates no value and occupies finite space.

## The Solution

### How Scrappr Works

Scrappr is a mobile-first web application connecting two distinct user types around a shared transaction: the transfer of scrap metal from someone who has it to someone who can monetize it.

| Role                 | Name     | What They Do                                            | What They Get                             |
| -------------------- | -------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Resident / Homeowner | Scrappee | Lists scrap metal with photo, category, and location    | Free removal, optional tip income         |
| Independent Hauler   | Scrappr  | Claims listings, picks up metal, delivers to scrap yard | Commodity income from yard payout         |
| Scrap Yard           | Partner  | Purchases metal by weight from Scrappr haulers          | Predictable volume, referral relationship |

### The Scrappee Experience

- Open Scrappr on any device, no app download required
- Photograph the metal item and select a category: appliances, copper, aluminum, cans, brass, steel, or electronics
- Add a brief description and confirm location via geolocation
- Receive a notification when a hauler claims the listing
- Mark complete when metal is gone. No payment required, ever

For residents dealing with renovation debris, old appliances, or accumulated scrap, Scrappr solves a specific problem: there is currently no low-friction path to dispose of metal items that have value but are too large or awkward for a standard recycling bin. Scrappr turns that friction into a solved workflow.

### The Scrappr Hauler Experience

- Browse nearby listings on an interactive map or list view
- Filter by material type, distance, and estimated value
- Claim a listing, locking it from other haulers for a set window
- Pick up the metal, deliver to a partner yard, collect payment by weight
- Mark the listing complete in the app

Haulers can string multiple pickups into a single route, maximizing payout per trip. The platform surfaces estimated value per listing based on material category and quantity, allowing haulers to route-optimize before they leave their driveway.

### Why Cans Are Included

Aluminum cans are the most frequently recycled item in the U.S. by rate, yet enormous volume still ends up in landfills due to lack of bin access, particularly in multifamily buildings. Individually, a can is worth fractions of a cent. A bag of 200 crushed cans is worth roughly $1.30 at $0.65 per pound.

Cans are included in Scrappr for two strategic reasons. First, they increase listing frequency. A resident who lists cans weekly becomes a habitual user. Habit drives the platform's long-term retention. Second, cans are the fill-the-truck category. A hauler running a route anchored by a water heater and copper pipe will take a bag of cans because the incremental weight adds to their yard payout at essentially zero additional cost.

## Revenue Model

Scrappr operates a dual-track monetization strategy combining predictable subscription revenue from haulers with volume-based revenue from scrap yard partnerships.

### Track 1: Hauler Subscription

Scrapprs pay a monthly platform fee for access to the listing feed, map tools, and route optimization features. This is the primary day-one revenue stream because it requires no third-party partnerships and scales directly with hauler acquisition.

| Tier  | Monthly Fee | Features                                                                    |
| ----- | ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Basic | $12/month   | Listing feed access, map view, claim up to 10 listings/month                |
| Pro   | $29/month   | Unlimited claims, route optimizer, material filters, priority notifications |
| Fleet | $79/month   | Multi-hauler management, analytics dashboard, bulk claim tools              |

At 200 active Pro haulers, the subscription track generates approximately $5,800 per month in recurring revenue. This is achievable in a single metro market with aggressive hauler acquisition.

### Track 2: Scrap Yard Partnership

Scrappr partners with local and regional scrap yards to drive hauler volume. Partner yards receive preferential routing in the app: when a hauler completes a pickup, Scrappr surfaces the nearest partner yard as the recommended drop-off. In exchange, partner yards pay Scrappr a per-pound referral fee or flat monthly access fee.

- **Referral fee model:** $0.01 to $0.02 per pound of metal routed through the platform to partner yards
- **Flat access model:** $200 to $500 per month for preferred routing status in a given radius
- **Data model (long-term):** aggregate supply intelligence sold to commodity traders or waste management operators

The yard partnership model aligns Scrappr's revenue with actual metal throughput. As the platform grows, so does the value of the routing relationship. A yard paying $300 per month for preferred status that generates 5 additional hauler deliveries per week is receiving significant value relative to cost.

### Why These Two Tracks Work Together

Subscriptions provide baseline MRR with no dependency on yard relationships. Yard partnerships provide upside that scales with volume and require no change to the Scrappee or Scrappr experience. The Scrappee always pays nothing. The Scrappr earns commodity income. Scrappr earns from both sides of the supply chain without touching the transaction itself.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Single Metro Launch (Months 1 to 6)

Launch in a single metro market to validate the two-sided marketplace and establish initial yard partnerships. Scrappee acquisition targets:

- Single-family homeowners via neighborhood platforms, community groups, and local social media
- Residents in high-density urban areas where bulk metal disposal options are limited
- Community organizations and neighborhood associations focused on local sustainability

Scrappr hauler acquisition targets:

- Existing independent scrap haulers and pickers already working the metro
- Gig workers currently offering free metal pickup informally through Craigslist and Facebook
- Small junk removal operators looking for a supplemental, no-overhead lead source

### Phase 2: Yard Partnership Activation (Months 3 to 9)

Approach 3 to 5 independent scrap yards in the metro for pilot partnership agreements. The partnership pitch is straightforward: Scrappr drives hauler volume to your yard. You pay a flat monthly fee for preferred routing. No technology integration required on the yard's end. Early yard partners benefit from first-mover advantage in their radius before competing yards can access the platform.

### Phase 3: Regional Expansion (Months 9 to 18)

Replicate the single-metro playbook in secondary markets across the Midwest and beyond. Each market requires local hauler acquisition and at least one yard partnership before launch. The platform itself is market-agnostic; the go-to-market is locally driven through grassroots hauler recruitment and direct yard outreach.

## Platform Category Rules and Guardrails

### Accepted Categories

Scrappr accepts listings in the following categories, aligned with standard scrap yard buy lists:

- **Aluminum:** cans, siding, extrusions, wire, radiators, wheels, cast
- **Copper:** pipe, wire (bare bright, #1, #2, insulated), sheet, breakage
- **Brass:** yellow brass, red brass, fittings, borings
- **Steel:** sheet metal, cast iron, prepared steel, appliances
- **Appliances:** washers, dryers, water heaters, furnaces, stoves, snowblowers, lawnmowers
- **Mixed metal:** general scrap not fitting a single category

### Blocked Categories

The following items are blocked at listing creation, consistent with industry-standard yard exclusions:

- Refrigerators, freezers, dehumidifiers (freon removal required, hazmat compliance)
- Microwaves (electronic waste classification)
- Alkaline, Li-Ion, Ni-Cd batteries (hazardous material)
- Propane tanks (explosion risk)
- E-waste: computers, circuit boards, TVs, light bulbs
- Tires and catalytic converters (theft concern, regulatory complexity)

When a resident attempts to list a blocked item, Scrappr redirects them to the appropriate disposal channel: county hazardous waste drop-off, e-waste recycling partner, or HVAC contractor for freon appliances. This keeps the user in the app and builds trust even when Scrappr cannot fulfill the request directly.

### Prep Requirements by Category

Certain categories require resident prep before a hauler can legally or safely transport them:

- **Lawnmowers and engines:** oil, gas, and coolant must be drained and caps removed
- **BBQ grills:** propane tanks must be removed and detached
- **Air conditioners:** must be labeled as evacuated or non-evacuated (affects yard pricing)

Scrappr surfaces these prep requirements as a checklist when residents create listings in applicable categories, reducing wasted hauler trips and improving pickup completion rates.

## Environmental Impact

Scrappr is an environmental platform disguised as an economic one. The environmental case is straightforward:

- Every pound of aluminum recycled saves the energy required to produce new aluminum from bauxite ore, a reduction of approximately 95% in energy consumption per ton
- Every pound of copper recycled avoids the mining, smelting, and refining process that produces significant greenhouse gas emissions and water contamination
- Every appliance diverted from a landfill eliminates a source of refrigerants, oils, and heavy metals that leach into groundwater over time

Unlike most environmental programs, Scrappr requires no behavioral change rooted in civic virtue. The incentive is economic. Scrapprs participate because they earn money. Scrappees participate because removal is free and effortless. The environmental benefit is the output of a transaction that both parties enter for entirely self-interested reasons.

This is the most durable kind of environmental program: one that does not need to convince anyone to care about the environment.

## Conclusion

The recycling system's core failure is not at the facility. It is at the household. Seventy-six percent of recyclable material never enters the system at all, not because people are indifferent, but because the friction of participation exceeds the perceived benefit.

Scrappr eliminates that friction for scrap metal specifically, a category with genuine commodity value, established yard infrastructure, and a hauler class already motivated by profit. The platform does not ask residents to care about recycling. It asks them to take a photo. The rest of the system does the work.

Monetized through hauler subscriptions and yard partnerships, Scrappr generates revenue without touching the resident transaction. The business model is self-reinforcing: more haulers drive more listings resolved, which drives more yard volume, which funds deeper yard partnerships, which improves the hauler experience.

### The Opportunity

A two-sided marketplace where one side pays nothing, one side earns real income, and the platform clips value from both the subscription and the supply chain. The environmental benefit is the byproduct of a transaction that would happen anyway.

Scrappr is the missing infrastructure between the household and the scrap yard. The metal is already there. The haulers are already working. The yards are already buying. Scrappr connects them.
